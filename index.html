WELCOME TO PurNET!

PurNET is Purdue University's Networks Research Group spanning the CS and ECE Departments.

PurNET brings together researchers and practitioners interested in the design, implementation, and evaluation of network systems and applications. These seminars seeks to act as a conduit for the sharing of recently published novel ides in support of performance, scalability, and security of Internet systems and applications, including Software Defined Networks (SDNs), Web searching and indexing, content distribution and delivery networks, Web services, online social networks, Internet of Things, mobile Web, and edge computing.

The topics of interest include, but are by no means limited to:

  • Software Defined Networks (SDN)
  • Network Abstractions, e.g., Knowledge Plane
  • Programmable Data Planes
  • Internet of things (Internet of Things)
  • Content delivery architectures
  • Peer-to-Peer systems
  • Support for mobile and wireless systems
  • Web caching and replication
  • Edge services and dynamic content delivery
  • Multimedia content distribution
  • Overlay networks
  • Content placement and request routing
  • Measurement studies of deployed systems
  • Security and privacy
  • Wide-area upload and content gathering
  • Novel web-based applications
  • Web/database integration
  • Information retrieval and searching
  • Electronic commerce
  • Internet telephony
  • Social computing
  • Internet of Things
  • Mobile web
  • Big web data
